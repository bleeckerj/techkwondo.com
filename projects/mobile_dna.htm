Mobile DNA is a proposal developed to develop a research program that would perform an ethnographic study of the Mobile Lifestyle. Presented at The Future of Wireless Technologies and Services at Fuji-Xerox PARC The study was to be performed in such a way as to yield insights into how particular groups of people transit, communicate with friends, colleagues, workmates, share knowledge, conduct business, and engage in entertainment over the course of several weeks. The precise group that would be studied would determined by the study underwriters.
This proposal was developed initially to help organizations determine how they might use mobile technology in ways that they might not consider without a rich understanding as to the intimate details of people's mobile lives.
